<p data-start="1718" data-end="1739"><strong data-start="1718" data-end="1739">Market Definition</strong></p>
<p data-start="1741" data-end="2395">Liquid synthetic rubber is a type of synthetic rubber that remains in liquid form at room temperature. It is produced by polymerizing monomers such as styrene, butadiene, and isoprene. The resulting material exhibits superior properties such as flexibility, durability, excellent resistance to weathering, and heat. Liquid synthetic rubber is used in a variety of industrial applications including automotive manufacturing (for tires and seals), construction (for coatings and waterproofing), adhesives, and coatings, among others. It provides strong bonding, long-lasting durability, and enhanced performance characteristics compared to other materials.</p>
